Cheesecake Recipe

Description

This was my first attempt at making a cheesecake. Online recipes like to warn you to be careful of the surface cracking, but my old and crappy oven didn't seem to have a problem. The water bath does most of the work. The springform pan is also an amazing invention.

Ultimately, this was a pretty easy recipe. I wasn't prepared for how rich it ended up being, though, so if I were to do it again, I'd use a smaller pan.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 18 graham crackers
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 stick butter, melted

For the filling:

  • 2 1/2 lbs cream cheese (ROOM TEMPERATURE!!!)
  • 1/8 tsp salt
  • 1 1/2 cups sugar
  • 1/2 cup sourcream
  • 2 tsp lemon juiice
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 5 whole eggs & 2 yolks

For the topping:

  • 12 ounces raspberries
  • 1/3 cup sugar
  • 2 tsp lemon juice

Steps

Crust:

  1. Preheat oven to 325° F.
  2. Sppray a 9-inch springform pan with nonstick cooking spray.
  3. Process crackers into dust. Add sugar and butter, then mix until moistened.
  4. Press cracker mixture into pan, making sure it comes one inch up the sides.
  5. Bake 12 minutes or until slightly browned. Pull out of oven and let cool.
  6. Cover the bottom and outside of the pan with foil. This is a barrier to keep the water out.

Filling:

  1. Beat room temperature cream cheese until softened.
  2. Add the salt and sugar, then beat until well combined.
  3. Add the sour cream, lemon juice, and vanilla. Beat to combine.
  4. Add eggs and yolks, one at a time. Beat to combine. Mixture should turn slight yellow color.

Baking:

  1. Pour filling mixture into pan until it just covers the crust.
  2. Put foil-covered pan into roasting pan. Fill up the roasting pan with boiling (212° F) water until it reaches halfway up the sides of the cheescake.
  3. Bake 1 1/2 hours then turn off oven and open the door a crack. Let sit 1 hour, then ttake out and put into fridge for at least 6 hours.

Finish:

  1. In a small saucepan, combine raspberries, lemon juice, and sugar. Add lemon zest (optional).
  2. Heat over medium heat until simmering.
  3. Remove from heat and puree until smooth. Run through strainer until seeds are gone and mixture is smooth.
  4. Let topping cool before putting on cheesecake.
  5. Decorate according to your tastes. Rustic (messy) is in style nowadays.
  6. Cut yourself a slice and enjoy!
